Objectives

There are four primary goals for the course. The student should be able to...

  1. Define science and explain.
  2. Define scientific model and explain.
  3. Deconstruct a scientific model.
  4. Construct a scientific model.

To achieve these goals the course will be divided into three sections. In each section the student should be able to...

Section 1: Definition

  1. Define science and explain.
  2. Discuss public perceptions of science.
  3. Define and discuss the scientific method.
  4. Approach a scientific problem with confidence.
  5. Identify and locate information relevant to an occurrence.
  6. Define scientific model and explain.

Section 2: Deconstruction

  1. Recognize and categorize different scientific models.
  2. State and define the categories of scientific models as developed in class.
  3. Assess the strengths and weaknesses of different categories of models.
  4. Deconstruct a model to explain its workings and assumptions.
  5. Explain what a particular model is best for and in what ways it fails.
  6. Identify the measurable characteristics associated with a scientific problem.
  7. Identify the characteristics of a model that may affect the result, and identify which should be maintained and which should be varied.

Section 3: Construction

  1. Amend a model that is shown to be inadequate.
  2. Discuss the development and growth of models.
  3. Conceptualize a suitable model to test a hypothesis.
  4. Construct an original model.

Lesson Outline

Each lecture/seminar runs for 110 min.

Section 1: Definition 

Week 1: Jan 4, 6

Lecture
Definition of science		Seminar
Good vs. bad science
  1. (30 min) Intro course and syllabus.
  2. (20 min) Public perception of science.
  3. Arrange students in to groups of 4.
  4. (15 min) Each group creates their own definition of science.
  5. (25 min) Discuss. Construct single acceptable definition.
  6. (20 min) What is not science? Discuss.
  1. Arrange students into groups of 4.
  2. (50 min) Each group finds 2 papers of good science in library.
  3. (30 min) Report on their papers. Why are they scientific?
  4. (20 min) Discussion: refine definition of science.
  5. Bad science.
  6. (10 min) Assignment 1 (due next lecture): Group finds 1 paper from peer-reviewed journals illustrating bad science and explains:
    1. 1/2 page single-sided, single-spaced. Summarize and explain why it's an example of bad science.
    2. Brief group presentation next lecture.

  1. (20 min) Assign groups (of 4) and topics (2 per group) for Project 1.

Week 11: Mar 22, 24

Lecture
KISS principle (Rik)		Seminar
Demonstrate rough/toy model
  1. (10 min) Divide class into groups (of 5).
  2. (10 min) Set up observation of unusual natural phenomenon
  3. Groups try to come up with explanation of observation
    1. (10 min) Groups come up with the simplest explanation they can think of.
    2. (10 min) Assume that the simple explanations have already been tested and found to be false. Come up with an elaborate explanation.
    3. (10 min) Groups write down (1 page for each explanation):
      1. A summary of their explanation,
      2. A detailed list of every single assumption it requires, and
      3. A probability estimate for each assumption (orders of magnitude).
    4. (10 min) Groups calculate total probability of observation given explanation.
  4. (20 min) Derive Bayes Theorem and odds ratio of two explanations.
  5. (20 min) Groups calculate odds ratio for their explanations.
  6. (10 min) Drive home the point: simpler is more likely to be true! (Occam's razor, KISS.)
  1. In LSK 303E (announce in advance!)
  2. Reviewed by group
110 min remaining.
